Let's reimagine beauty.

How do young women perceive themselves? What are the social, financial, and medical costs of poor self-perception?



Now more than ever, girls and young women feel pressured to be beautiful.




Source: The 2017 Dove Global Girls Beauty and Confidence Report (Diedrichs and Weiner, 2017)

These pressures have been costly for women and society at large, financially, socially, and medically.






The financial and non-financial costs of dissatisfaction with women's bodies has been substantial.

Source: The Real Cost of Beauty Ideals (Dove, 2022)




Medically, the impact on women has been two-fold.

Women are driven by body dissatisfaction to elective cosmetic procedures. These non-surgical and surgical procedures can cause unnecessary harm.

Source: International Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgery (ISAPS) International Survey On Aesthetic/Cosmetic Procedures
(ISAPS, 2010; ISAPS, 2013; ISAPS, 2015; ISAPS, 2017; ISAPS, 2019; ISAPS, 2021)

Next, women can suffer irreparable harm from low self-esteem, causing mental disorders like eating disorders or depression.



Source: The Real Cost of Beauty Ideals (Dove, 2022)

The increased pressures faced by women could be due to increased social media and other media consumption like television shows.


Source:

To this day, it seems the body positivity movement has not reached reality TV, which continues to be filled with scenes of women bodyshaming other women, rather than celebrating their differences.

What can we do?


Research has shown that increased media literacy can help women recognise augmentation of images online, which can prevent harmful and unhealthy comparisons between themselves and public figures like models or influencers.
